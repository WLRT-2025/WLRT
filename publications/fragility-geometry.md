# Fragility Geometry in Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT)

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19416731

---

## Overview

This paper introduces a geometric framework for representing fragility within Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT). It builds upon previously defined fragility metrics and formalizes the structural space in which these quantities exist and interact.

The framework defines fragility as a distributed property over a space of liquidity configurations, rather than as an isolated scalar quantity.

---

## Key Contribution

The paper establishes the concept of **fragility geometry**, introducing:

- a **state space of liquidity configurations**  
- a **fragility field** defined over this space  
- **stable and fragile regions** determined by structural conditions  
- **boundaries** defined via compensability (LSM)  
- **connectivity** enabling structural propagation of instability  
- **admissible transformations** of system states  
- **invariants** preserved under structural changes  

The framework is strictly structural and does not include time dynamics, estimation procedures, or control mechanisms.

---

## Role within WLRT

This work defines the **geometric layer** of WLRT and extends the metric foundation established in the previous paper.

It provides the structural basis for:

- **Topology** — higher-order organization of connectivity  
- **Inverse Problems** — reconstruction of system state from observations  
- **Control** — admissible transformations within the system
