# Fragility Metrics in Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT)

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19313950

---

## Overview

This paper introduces the foundational metric layer of Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT), defining a minimal set of structural variables required to describe fragility in liquidity-driven systems.

The framework establishes three core metrics:

- **Local Fragility Index (LFI)** — a measure of local structural instability  
- **Local Stability Margin (LSM)** — a measure of compensability and distance to instability boundaries  
- **Fragility Field Index (FFI)** — a descriptor of structural connectivity and propagation potential  

Together, these metrics provide a consistent and non-stochastic representation of fragility as an intrinsic property of liquidity configurations.

---

## Key Contribution

The paper formalizes fragility as a structural, rather than probabilistic, characteristic of financial systems. It introduces a minimal metric system that:

- does not rely on statistical estimation  
- is independent of time parametrization  
- is defined directly on liquidity configurations  
- captures both local instability and systemic propagation  

This formulation enables a shift from price-based modeling to liquidity-based structural analysis.

---

## Role within WLRT

This work defines the **metric layer** of WLRT and serves as the entry point to the fragility framework.

It provides the foundation for subsequent developments:

- **Fragility Geometry** — structural organization of fragility in state space  
- **Topology** — connectivity and higher-order structure  
- **Inverse Problems** — reconstruction of system state  
- **Control** — admissible transformations and interventions
