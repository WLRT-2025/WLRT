# WLRT Core Statements

## Structural Principles

Price is not the driver of markets.  
Liquidity is.

Price is not a signal.  
It is a consequence of liquidity redistribution.

Markets do not move because of patterns.  
Patterns appear because markets have already moved.

Volatility is not random.  
It is a result of structural fragility in the liquidity environment.

## Core Definition

Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT) is a framework for understanding markets as liquidity-driven environments.

In this framework, price is a derived variable — it emerges from the redistribution of liquidity across the system.

Market behavior is determined by how liquidity is allocated, concentrated, or removed.

## Interpretation Layer

Understanding markets means understanding liquidity — not interpreting price.
