# Open Problems — WLRT v5.0

## Status

This document collects open theoretical and mathematical problems related to WLRT v5.0.

The purpose of this file is not to provide final answers, but to define the active research frontier.

---

## Open Problem 1

### What is the complete architectural state of an adaptive system?

Preliminary variables:

- V — total system value;
- S — strategic contour;
- W — working contour;
- P — created value;
- D — withdrawn profit;
- I — external capital inflow;
- M — market revaluation of the working contour.

The question is whether these variables are sufficient to describe the architectural state of a system, or whether additional state variables are required.

---

## Open Problem 2

### Is lambda fundamental?

Preliminary definition:

\[
\lambda = \frac{W}{S}
\]

where:

- W — working contour;
- S — strategic contour.

The question is whether lambda is a fundamental invariant, a derived architectural coefficient, or only a local diagnostic ratio.

---

## Open Problem 3

### Can system value increase while working liquidity decreases?

The BitCapital experiment suggests that the following configuration may occur:

\[
\Delta P > 0
\]

\[
\Delta S > 0
\]

\[
\Delta V > 0
\]

while

\[
\Delta W < 0
\]

This means that a system may become more valuable while simultaneously losing part of its ability to continue redistribution.

The question is whether this is a special case or a general architectural phenomenon.

---

## Open Problem 4

### What is the general invariant of adaptive liquidity systems?

If lambda is not fundamental, a more general invariant may be required.

The question is whether adaptive systems preserve:

- working capacity;
- redistribution capacity;
- strategic-to-working balance;
- phase stability;
- or another, more general architectural property.

---

## Open Problem 5

### How should profit withdrawal be modeled?

A system may generate profit and withdraw part of it.

The question is how to define a mathematically stable withdrawal rule that does not destroy working liquidity.

---

## Open Problem 6

### What is the difference between growth and development?

A system may increase in total value without improving its architecture.

The question is how to distinguish:

- value growth;
- architectural development;
- liquidity expansion;
- strategic accumulation;
- working degradation.

---

## Open Problem 7

### What are the laws of Adaptive System Architecture?

The current hypothesis is that Adaptive System Architecture may require its own laws, separate from the fundamental laws of WLRT.

These laws should describe:

- preservation of working liquidity;
- balance between strategic and working contours;
- sustainable scaling;
- safe withdrawal;
- adaptive stability.
