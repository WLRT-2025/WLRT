# System State

## Status

Provisional Canonical v0.1

---

## Definition

A System State is the complete architectural description of an adaptive system at a specific moment.

The state is determined not only by the values of the canonical variables but also by their structural relationships.

---

## Properties

A System State is:

- dynamic;
- multidimensional;
- architecture-dependent;
- environment-dependent;
- measurable in principle.

---

## Current Components

The current research assumes that a System State includes at least:

- V
- CW
- CA
- P
- D
- I
- M

Future research may introduce additional canonical variables.

---

## Important Observation

Different systems may possess identical values of V while occupying different architectural states.

Therefore, Total System Value alone is insufficient for describing adaptive systems.

---

## Current Status

The exact mathematical representation of System State remains an open research problem.
