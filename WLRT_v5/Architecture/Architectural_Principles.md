# Architectural Principles

## Status

Canonical Draft v0.1

This document introduces the first architectural principles identified during the development of the architectural branch of Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT) v5.0.

These principles describe the organization of adaptive systems rather than mathematical laws.

They may later become the foundation for formal architectural laws.

---

# Introduction

The principles presented here summarize the structural observations obtained from the study of adaptive systems.

They do not replace the fundamental laws of WLRT.

Instead, they describe how adaptive systems should organize their internal resources in order to operate sustainably under those laws.

---

# Principle 1

## Configuration Principle

The ability of an adaptive system to continue redistribution is determined by the configuration of its resources rather than by their total quantity.

Equal system value does not imply equal redistribution capability.

---

# Principle 2

## Functional Assignment Principle

Resources are not permanently assigned to architectural objects.

Their role is determined by the function they perform within the current state of the system.

The same resource may participate in different architectural configurations during different redistribution cycles.

---

# Principle 3

## Working Configuration Principle

Working Configuration exists to execute the current redistribution cycle.

Its purpose is operational continuity rather than long-term preservation.

---

# Principle 4

## Architectural Configuration Principle

Architectural Configuration exists to preserve, restore and develop the Working Configuration across future redistribution cycles.

Its purpose is architectural continuity.

---

# Principle 5

## Reproduction Principle

Every completed redistribution cycle should preserve the conditions required for the emergence of the next Working Configuration.

Failure to reproduce these conditions leads to gradual architectural degradation even when total system value continues to increase.

---

# Principle 6

## Configuration Transition Principle

Adaptive systems continuously redistribute resources between architectural configurations.

The transition of resources between configurations is a normal property of adaptive systems and should not be interpreted as a structural anomaly.

---

# Current Status

The principles presented here are considered canonical architectural principles of the current research stage.

Their mathematical formalization and possible transformation into architectural laws remain subjects of future research.
