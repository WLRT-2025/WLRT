# Empirical Cases

This directory contains empirical applications of **Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT)**.

Unlike the core theoretical publications, these documents analyze real-world systems and events using the concepts and diagnostic framework developed in WLRT.

The objective of these case studies is not to predict specific events or dates, but to identify structural vulnerabilities, compensating mechanisms, phase transitions and early warning indicators within complex adaptive systems.

---

## Current Case Studies

### Russia (2025–2026)

Analysis of the structural evolution of Russia from the perspective of WLRT.

Focus areas include:

- systemic resilience;

- crisis transition mechanisms;
- early warning indicators.

## Purpose

These case studies demonstrate how WLRT can be applied outside theoretical models to diagnose the evolution of real complex systems.

Each case is intended to illustrate:

- structural analysis rather than event forecasting;
- redistribution dynamics rather than isolated indicators;
- phase transitions rather than single economic variables;
- compensating mechanisms rather than individual policy decisions.

---

## Relation to WLRT v5.0

Comparative analysis of different countries revealed structural similarities that could not be fully explained using only financial liquidity.

These observations initiated the WLRT v5.0 research program on **Generalized Redistribution Dynamics**, extending the study of liquidity redistribution toward broader redistribution processes in complex adaptive systems.
