# WLRT: Practical Perspective

Most market approaches attempt to predict price.

WLRT takes a different approach.

It does not try to forecast where the price will go.  
Instead, it determines **what is structurally possible**.

---

## The Core Shift

Instead of asking:

“Where will the price go?”

WLRT reframes the problem:

**What states are admissible,  
and what transitions are structurally allowed?**

---

## What This Enables

This shift leads to a different type of capability:

- identifying environments where decisions are structurally viable,  
- excluding situations where observable signals are misleading,  
- operating within constrained transition spaces,  
- and avoiding structurally unstable configurations.

---

## Not Prediction — Selection

WLRT does not provide predictive signals.

It provides **selection criteria**.

Instead of trying to anticipate outcomes,  
it filters the space of possible actions.

---

## Practical Effect

In practice, this results in:

- reduced exposure to structurally unstable regimes,  
- improved consistency of decisions,  
- and controlled interaction with system dynamics.

---

## Why It Works

Most models operate on observable data.

WLRT operates on **structural constraints of the system**.

This difference allows it to detect situations  
where standard approaches fail.

---

## Implementation

Applied WLRT systems are implementation-specific  
and are not fully disclosed.

They are based on:

- state identification,  
- admissible transitions,  
- and controlled execution within structural limits.

---

## Summary

WLRT does not attempt to predict the future.

It defines the boundaries within which the future can unfold.

Working within these boundaries  
is what creates practical advantage.
