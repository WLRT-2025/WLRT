# Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT)

Financial markets are typically described in terms of price.  
WLRT starts from a different assumption:

**price is not a fundamental variable.**

It is an observable manifestation of a deeper structure.

---

## Core Statement

A market is not a process of price change.  
It is a system of liquidity redistribution.

Price emerges as a projection of the system’s state in the observable space.

---

## What Changes

If liquidity is the state and price is the observation, then:

- market behavior is not governed by price levels,  
- but by the structure of liquidity distribution,  

- price movements are not primary,  
- they reflect internal redistribution processes,  

- observable dynamics do not contain complete information about the system.

---

## Implication

Future system states cannot be uniquely inferred from local observations.

Any short-term movement admits multiple admissible continuations.

The market is not deterministic in the observable space.

---

## What WLRT Does

WLRT does not attempt to predict price.

Instead, it:

- defines admissible system states,  
- constrains the space of possible transitions,  
- identifies zones of structural fragility,  
- establishes boundaries of what can occur.

---

## Practical Meaning

Instead of asking  
“where will the price go?”,  

the relevant question becomes:

**what state the system is in  
and which transitions are admissible.**

---

## Why It Matters

Most models operate on observable variables.  
WLRT operates on what generates them.

This shifts analysis from interpretation  
to structure.
