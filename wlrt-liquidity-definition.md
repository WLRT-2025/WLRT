## Short Definition

Liquidity is the structural capacity of a system to absorb and redistribute transactional flows without generating instability.
# Liquidity — WLRT Definition

In the Wave Liquidity Redistribution Theory (WLRT), liquidity is not defined as the ability to convert assets into cash.

Liquidity is defined as the structural capacity of a system to absorb and redistribute transactional flows without generating instability.

In this framework:

• Liquidity is a property of the environment, not of individual assets  
• Liquidity determines how transactions propagate through the system  
• Price is a derived variable that emerges from liquidity redistribution  
• Instability arises when liquidity distribution becomes structurally imbalanced  

Thus, liquidity is not a measure of tradability, but a fundamental parameter governing system behavior.
